Bike Anatomy

  • Frame & Fork
    • Function: Supports rider, components, and luggage.
    • Material: Steel and aluminum alloys, titanium, and carbon fiber.
    • Uses: commuting, leisure, travel, road, and racing.
    • Construction: Rigid or foldable.
  • Wheels & Tires
    • Function: Keep you rolling forward
    • Material: Steel, Aluminum, Carbon Fiber
    • Component Parts: Rim, Spoke, Hub, Skewer.
    • Tires: thin for racing; thick for offroad.
    • Gear Sprocket
  • Seat & Seat Post
    • Function: keeps the rider in correct riding position.

      • Seat Post

    • Materials: Steel, Aluminum, Carbon-fiber.
    • Adjustability: up and down; pitch

      • Seat

    • Materials: Leather, synthetic cover, foam, plastic.
    • Adjustability: Sliding rails for front to back movement.
    • Male v. Female: Male seat is narrower and longer.
  • The Cockpit

      Handlebar

      Control center of the bike:

    • Uses: hand placement, steering, braking, gear shifting, lights, bell, navigation, etc.
    • Tip: the longer the rides, the more hand positions you want to have.
    • Types: Short, straight, beach, curved, mustache, bullhorn, drop, trekking.
    • Grip: rubber, leather, cork, foam.

      • Stem

      Holds the handlebar and adjusts its position.

    • Types: threaded (quill), threadless
    • Materials: Aluminum, carbon fiber
    • Adjustment: height and pitch

      • Brakes and Brake Levers

      Help you stop the bike.

    • Brake Types: Skid; Coaster-Brake, Cantilever, Single Pivot, Dual Pivot, Disk.
    • Lever Types: full size, short lever, brakes with built-in gear shifters.
    • Hoods covers: rubber components that make holding the brake lever easier.
  • Pedals & Crank
    • Function: Feet rotate the crank that moves the chain
    • Types: Single sided; Double-sided; Caged; Clips
